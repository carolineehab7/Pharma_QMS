Document Control

Critical Process

Overview

Document Control ensures that all quality-related documents are properly created, reviewed, approved, distributed, and maintained throughout their lifecycle. This process is fundamental to GMP compliance and regulatory requirements.

Key Components

  • Document Creation: Standardized templates and authoring procedures
  • Review & Approval: Multi-level review workflow with electronic signatures
  • Version Control: Automated versioning with change tracking
  • Distribution: Controlled access and distribution to authorized personnel
  • Archival: Long-term retention per regulatory requirements

Process Flow

graph LR A[Document Request] --> B[Draft Creation] B --> C[Technical Review] C --> D{Approved?} D -->|No| B D -->|Yes| E[QA Review] E --> F{Approved?} F -->|No| B F -->|Yes| G[Final Approval] G --> H[Distribution] H --> I[Training] I --> J[Implementation]

Compliance Requirements

21 CFR Part 11: Electronic records and signatures
ICH Q10: Pharmaceutical quality system

Change Control

High Impact

Overview

Change Control is a systematic approach to managing all changes to processes, equipment, materials, and documentation. It ensures that changes are properly evaluated, approved, implemented, and verified.

Change Categories

Minor Changes

Low impact, expedited approval

Fast Track
Major Changes

Significant impact, full assessment

Standard Review
Critical Changes

High risk, extensive validation

Extended Review

Impact Assessment Areas

  • Product Quality & Safety
  • Regulatory Compliance
  • Validation Status
  • Documentation Requirements
  • Training Needs
  • Supply Chain Impact

Deviation Management

Critical Process

Overview

Deviation Management handles any departure from approved procedures, specifications, or standards. This process ensures proper investigation, root cause analysis, and implementation of corrective and preventive actions.

Quality Control Testing

Core Process

Overview

Quality Control Testing ensures that raw materials, in-process materials, and finished products meet established specifications and quality standards.

Testing Categories

Raw Material Testing
  • Identity testing
  • Purity analysis
  • Potency determination
  • Microbial testing
Finished Product Testing
  • Assay and content uniformity
  • Dissolution testing
  • Stability testing
  • Sterility testing (if applicable)

OOS/OOT Investigations

Out-of-Specification (OOS) and Out-of-Trend (OOT) results require thorough investigation:

Batch Record Review

GMP Critical

Overview

Batch Record Review is a systematic examination of all production and control records to ensure compliance with approved procedures and specifications before batch release.

Review Checkpoints

‚úì Documentation Completeness

All required signatures, dates, and entries present

‚úì Material Reconciliation

Input vs. output quantities within acceptable limits

‚úì Process Parameters

All critical parameters within specifications

‚úì Deviations & Investigations

All deviations documented and resolved

Release Criteria

  • All QC test results meet specifications
  • Batch record complete and accurate
  • No unresolved deviations
  • Environmental monitoring acceptable
  • Equipment qualification current

Training Management

Personnel Development

Overview

Training Management ensures that all personnel are properly qualified and trained to perform their assigned duties in accordance with GMP requirements.

Training Categories

GMP Training

Basic and advanced GMP principles

SOP Training

Procedure-specific training

On-the-Job Training

Practical skills development

Training Lifecycle

  • Needs Assessment: Identify training requirements
  • Training Delivery: Classroom, e-learning, or hands-on
  • Competency Assessment: Verify understanding and skills
  • Documentation: Maintain training records
  • Periodic Retraining: Refresh knowledge and skills
