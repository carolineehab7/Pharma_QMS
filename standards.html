Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP)

21 CFR Part 211

Overview

GMP is a system for ensuring that products are consistently produced and controlled according to quality standards. It is designed to minimize the risks involved in pharmaceutical production that cannot be eliminated through testing the final product.

Key GMP Principles

1. Quality Management

Comprehensive quality system covering all aspects of production

2. Personnel

Qualified and trained staff with defined responsibilities

3. Premises & Equipment

Suitable facilities and properly maintained equipment

4. Documentation

Clear procedures and accurate record-keeping

5. Production

Controlled processes following approved procedures

6. Quality Control

Testing and verification of materials and products

21 CFR Part 211 Subparts

Subpart Topic Key Requirements
A General Provisions Scope and definitions
B Organization & Personnel Qualifications, training, hygiene
C Buildings & Facilities Design, maintenance, sanitation
D Equipment Design, maintenance, cleaning
E Control of Components Receipt, testing, storage
F Production & Process Controls Written procedures, in-process controls
G Packaging & Labeling Controls and inspection
I Laboratory Controls Testing and specifications
J Records & Reports Documentation requirements
K Returned & Salvaged Products Handling procedures

ISO Standards

International Standards

ISO 9001: Quality Management Systems

ISO 9001 provides a framework for quality management applicable to any organization. It focuses on meeting customer requirements and enhancing satisfaction.

Seven Quality Management Principles
1. Customer Focus

Meeting customer requirements and exceeding expectations

2. Leadership

Establishing unity of purpose and direction

3. Engagement of People

Competent, empowered, and engaged people

4. Process Approach

Understanding and managing interrelated processes

5. Improvement

Continual improvement as a permanent objective

6. Evidence-based Decision Making

Decisions based on analysis of data and information

7. Relationship Management

Managing relationships with interested parties

ISO 13485: Medical Devices QMS

Specifies requirements for a quality management system where an organization needs to demonstrate its ability to provide medical devices and related services that consistently meet customer and applicable regulatory requirements.

Certification Status: Our facility maintains ISO 9001:2015 and ISO 13485:2016 certifications with annual surveillance audits.

ICH Guidelines

International Harmonization

Overview

The International Council for Harmonisation of Technical Requirements for Pharmaceuticals for Human Use (ICH) brings together regulatory authorities and pharmaceutical industry to discuss scientific and technical aspects of drug registration.

Quality Guidelines (Q Series)

Guideline Title Focus Area
Q7 Good Manufacturing Practice for Active Pharmaceutical Ingredients API production quality
Q8 Pharmaceutical Development Quality by Design (QbD)
Q9 Quality Risk Management Risk-based approach
Q10 Pharmaceutical Quality System Integrated QMS
Q11 Development and Manufacture of Drug Substances API lifecycle
Q12 Technical and Regulatory Considerations for Pharmaceutical Product Lifecycle Management Post-approval changes

ICH Q9: Quality Risk Management

Provides principles and examples of tools for quality risk management that can be applied to different aspects of pharmaceutical quality.

Risk Assessment
  • Risk identification
  • Risk analysis
  • Risk evaluation
Risk Control
  • Risk reduction
  • Risk acceptance
Risk Communication
  • Risk review

ICH Q10: Pharmaceutical Quality System

Describes a comprehensive model for an effective pharmaceutical quality system based on ISO quality concepts and includes applicable GMP regulations.

Key Elements:
  • Process performance and product quality monitoring
  • Corrective and preventive action (CAPA) system
  • Change management
  • Management review of process performance and product quality
